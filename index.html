Sanitary Bakery

DAGUPAN CITY—An old “pugon” (wood-fired oven) inside a brick and concrete chamber at the back of a 75-year-old bakery in this city, the economic hub of Pangasinan province, has withstood the test of time, narrowly surviving a magnitude 7.7 earthquake in 1990 and widespread flooding in 2009.

The shop’s owner, Conchita Seen, believes that the pugon is the bakery’s secret in producing tasty “pan de sal,” loaf bread, and varieties of pastries and cookies that make Dagupeños rise early and queue every morning.

It was on August 13, 1947, or two months after Dagupan was granted cityhood on June 30 that year, when Sanitary Bakery opened its doors on Torres Bugallon Street, which was already renamed A. B. Fernandez Street.

ABOUT US

Sanitary Bakery, founded in 1947 in Dagupan, is one of the city's oldest and most beloved establishments. It aims to preserve the traditional flavors of old Dagupan by using a wood-fired oven, or "pugon," which has been in operation since 1952. The bakery focuses on offering quality, affordable products that evoke nostalgia, with their pan de sal being a particular favorite. Despite the growth of modern bakeries, Sanitary Bakery has remained true to its roots, keeping its simple yet authentic approach. Their commitment to traditional baking methods and preserving local heritage has made them a cherished part of the community.
